Runner's Lunge to Balance - Repeat on Each Side

1-Stand with your feet hip-width apart and your core engaged. Take a big step back with your left foot, and drop into a very low lunge by bending your right knee to 90 degrees and lowering your chest toward the floor to tap your left fingertips to the floor. Your extended left leg may be a little bent, but it shouldn't come to 90 degrees like a reverse lunge. 2-From this low runner's lunge position, push off your left foot and lift your left leg to come to a standing balance on your right leg with your left knee at hip height. 3-Pause for a moment in this balance, then immediately bring your left foot behind you and drop into another low lunge. 4-Do all of the reps on the same side, then repeat on the other side.

10 mins

Mountain Climber

1-Start in high plank, with your wrists under your shoulders, legs extended behind you, and your core engaged. 2-Engage your core and draw your right knee to your chest. 3-Return to your starting position and immediately draw your left knee to your chest. 4-Return your left leg to your starting position. 5-Continue to alternate, moving quickly.

10 mins

Corkscrew

1-Start in a high plank position with your wrists directly under your shoulders, core engaged, hips level, and legs extended straight behind you. 2-Lift your right hand and left foot a few inches off the floor. Swing your left foot under and across your body, allowing your body to naturally rotate in that direction, and tap your right hand to your left toes. 3-Return to your starting high plank position and repeat on the other side. 4-Make it easier: The closer you walk your feet in and the more you lift your hips, the easier this move will become. You can also move slowly, and keep your knees slightly bent throughout. If your flexibility does not allow you to tap your toes, tap your shin or knee instead.

10 mins
